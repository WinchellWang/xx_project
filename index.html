Modernizing Canada’s Lagoon Infrastructure

Clean, Scalable Nanobubble-Assisted Treatment for Freshwater Health and Climate Resilience

Project Overview

This project is developing an innovative, low-energy, and climate-resilient nanobubble-assisted treatment platform to improve the performance of lagoon-based wastewater systems across Canada. The project is specifically designed to help lagoon systems achieve better nutrient and organic matter removal while improving treatment reliability under Canadian operating conditions.

By combining cold-tolerant biology with advanced physical treatment, we provide a sustainable upgrade pathway for the small, rural, and northern communities that rely on these systems.

Why This Matters

Lagoon systems are widely used in Canada because they are affordable and simple to operate. However, many face significant challenges, especially during cold seasons, including:

  • Poor nutrient removal and phosphorus release.
  • Increased risks of algal blooms.
  • Difficulty meeting effluent requirements during seasonal transitions.

Key Innovations

Biological Consortium

Development of a cold-tolerant native algal-bacterial consortium tailored for Canadian wastewater conditions.

Advanced Treatment

Integration of air nanobubble-assisted biological treatment and biodegradable coagulation to lower energy and chemical demand.

Predictive Modeling

Creation of an ice-capable 3D predictive model to evaluate performance and support future scale-up.

Current Status

Advanced Development / Pre-Pilot

The project is well beyond the concept stage and is actively preparing for pilot-scale validation using real wastewater. Current work includes:

  • Enrichment of the cold-tolerant algal-bacterial consortium.
  • Optimization of nanobubble-assisted biological treatment and coagulation.
  • Ongoing laboratory development and process optimization.

Partnerships

This project is led by Queen’s University in collaboration with Fleming College’s Centre for Advancement of Water and Wastewater Technologies (CAWT). We also engage with municipal partners and community stakeholders for wastewater sampling and pilot validation.
